CARDS National Library Platform

Multi-Tenant Design System for Czech Academic Libraries SaaS Platform

Target 1/3

How can we build a multi-tenant, multi-brand, platform-agnostic design system for a national library infrastructure project serving research and academic institutions across the Czech Republic, modernizing library systems frozen since the 1990s while ensuring scalability and consistency across diverse institutional needs?

My role 2/3

The gist of it

As Design Systems Lead at the National Technical Library, I am building the design and research capabilities for the CARDS project‚Äîa CZK 1.37 billion (~$60M) national infrastructure initiative. I'm creating a multi-tenant, multi-brand, platform-agnostic design system for a SaaS library platform that will serve academic and research libraries across the Czech Republic through 2028.

More details

The CARDS (Czech Academic and Research Discovery Services) project represents a fundamental modernization of Czech academic library infrastructure. When I joined the National Technical Library in December 2023, the project had already begun its ambitious six-year timeline, with the goal of replacing outdated library systems‚Äîmany frozen in the 1990s‚Äîwith a modern, unified Next Generation Platform (NGP).

My primary responsibility is building a comprehensive design system that can serve multiple library institutions, each with their own brand identity and specific needs, while maintaining consistency and usability across the entire platform. This multi-tenant architecture requires careful consideration of white-labeling, theming capabilities, and component flexibility that traditional single-brand design systems don't face.

Beyond the design system, I'm establishing research capabilities within the organization‚Äîbringing user-centered design practices to a sector that has historically been technology-driven rather than user-driven. This involves creating processes for user research, usability testing, and continuous feedback loops with librarians, researchers, and general users who will interact with the platform.

The technical challenge is significant: the platform must work seamlessly across devices (addressing decades of poor mobile library experiences), integrate with existing national infrastructure including ORCID, DOI, and ISSN systems, and support complex library workflows from cataloging to electronic resource management to discovery services. The design system must be platform-agnostic to ensure longevity and flexibility as technology evolves over the project's remaining years.

I'm collaborating with the Service Centre team, participating libraries, and technical implementation partners to ensure the design system supports not just the initial platform launch but the ongoing evolution and expansion planned through 2028. This includes establishing governance processes for design contributions and maintaining design system documentation that empowers both internal teams and external library partners.

Artifacts 3/3

  • Multi-tenant, multi-brand design system architecture in Figma supporting white-labeling and institutional customization for participating Czech academic libraries
  • Platform-agnostic component library for library management systems, discovery services, and electronic resource management interfaces
  • Responsive design patterns ensuring mobile-first functionality‚Äîaddressing decades of library systems' poor mobile experiences
  • User research framework and methodologies for continuous improvement of library services and user experience
  • Design system documentation and governance processes for managing contributions across distributed library network
  • Integration specifications for national metadata infrastructure (ORCID, DOI, ISSN, Czech Core Metadata Model)
  • SaaS platform UX patterns for centralized cataloging, union catalog, unified search, and service center operations
