90's–style crm portal needed a refresh

Problem 1/4

How might we improve InPortál for financial advisers to give them an overview into their business?

Barriers 2/4

A young fintech company in advisery business uses multiple portals and websites to manage their clients. The advisers themselves use two of the portals, but InPortál is more focused on the company's needs, than those of its clients. (The other is newer and made for the advisers from the start.)
It also looks more like a simple spreadsheet, than a complex portal, and suffers from immense technological debt.
This is an ideal situation for a comprehensive progressive redesign of this portal. Goal of the project is to improve the quality of work for hundreths of brokers and in turn, thousands of their clients.

Early sketch of the new dashboard

Experience Map of the Client

Customer Journey Map of the Advisor

Process 3/4

  1. Review of current state, heuristic analysis & inventory of datatypes and sources.
  2. Experience mapping, Customer Journey Mapping & Service Blueprinting.
  3. Sketching on paper, then in Figma, then in code.
  4. Extra care on progressive steps of the redesign, so current practices translate into the renovated ui with as little friction as possible.
  5. Testing on users.
  6. More iterations and testing.

Dashboard showing the right data, so you don't even have to search for it

"Clients" view with insights and tabbed table

Result 4/4

First responses to surveys and answers from exploratory inteviews suggest, that the advisers feel a lack of insights into the trove of data they have at their disposal. The redesign focuses on these insights, rather than simply displaying the data.
The advisers also expressed a need for customisation. A tree test for the dashboard came back with no conclusion (done on 10 subjects with 50% completion rate).
The project is planned for completion in the winter of 2018.

The project would benefit from more testing and quantitative analyses.

Client: In Investments

Project length: WIP (expected 6 months)

ngo project needed a website

Problem 1/4

How might we explain the project's goal to help it reach public officials?

A sketch of the proposed page

Barriers 2/4

A friend of mine who's a social anthropologist asked me to make her a website for this project she's been working on for past few years. The project has a lot of audio & video material and a clear purpose, but lacks any kind of digital presence. It's usually presented at public events and exhibitions with follow–ups on Facebook.
The project has a very limited budget.

Process 3/4

  1. Review of options to publish and maintain a simple single–page website.
  2. Rapid prototyping, peer critique and 5–second tests.
  3. Website prototype in code.
  4. Release, more testing and evaluation.
  5. Ongoing iteration and promotion.

Final website made in VS Code, hosted for free. Click through for the live site!

Result 4/4

According to Google Analytics, the website was visited by every other visitor of the exphibition it was first mentioned on. It has reached about 5× as much supporters through Facebook.

The project requires minimal maintenance and the simplicity of it has inspired the researcher herself to learn to code, so she can take care of it in the future.

Client: Barbora Bírová

Location: Prague

Project length: 2 weeks

Problem 1/4

How can we redesign heo for managers, so they are incentived to lead their companies more effectively?
&
How can we redesign heo for operators to perform their daily routines more effectively?

Process of redesigning one of the interface elements

Barriers 2/4

The goal of this project was to redesign an erp software package to make it attractive to potential customers, while making it easy to use for current users. While the application is robust and reliable, its interface has become bloated and hard to use over the years.
Another challenge was that on this redesign a much bigger design change, in the corporation itself, was to be evaluated. So the project team had to take into account the whole portfolio, which consisted of a bunch of different software suites.

Process 3/4

  1. Stakeholder Analysis
  2. Business Model Canvas
  3. Target Audience Identification
  4. Partnership Model
  5. Usability Field Studies
  6. Usability Testing with 6 humans
  7. Co–design workshops with corporate stakeholders
  8. Heuristic Evaluation
  9. Prototyping
  10. Wireflows

Wireflow of the proposed screens for one of the prototypes

Result 4/4

The obvious solution was (Microsoft Office's) the Ribbon. So at the agency we conducted research, designed prototypes and tested it all. We also added the Dashboard to provide an overview of the most important views each user needs.

Client: cee leader in sme erps (confidential)

Location: CZ + SK

Project length: 1 year (participated 4 months)
