              <p class="blockquote">
                How might we help people save money — for things they usually borrow for?
              </p>

              <p>
                Obvious solution for savings now are <abbr title="Robotic, ie. automatic, not human">robo</abbr>
                advisers.
                The problem is, they still <a href="https://www.wired.com/story/beware-roboadvisors-wealthfront-betterment/"
                   rel="noopener noreferrer"
                   class="font-weight-bold text-primary">don't work</a>. What does, is traditional, empathetic,
                realistic,
                face 2 face human advisers. So instead of making another <em>Nutmeg</em> or <em>Betterment</em>, we decided
                to improve
                the rocky parts of client–adviser relationship.
              </p>

              <p>
                We did that by simplifying discovery of client's goals
                for the adviser. Necessary paperwork was also improved, by making it (finally) digital and easy to use.
                Because the best time to start saving was yesterday.
              </p>

              <p>
                Why did we not make another robo advisor? We think the Czech market is not ready for it yet. Financial
                literacy and technology adoption rates are low. There already are robo advisers available, for those
                who can use them. Who really needs help, are those who cannot, the uneducated and less fortunate. They
                are more
                often in debt or under distraint.
              </p>
